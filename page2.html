- how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

A good idea would be to take a close look at the solution(s) and ask yourself a few questions:

  1. Is it compatible with my current solution(s)?
  2. Does it do everything I need it to do?
  3. Is it better than my current solution?

SOME IMPORTANT FACTORS TO CONSIDER WHEN BUYING IT SYSTEMS AND SOFTWARE

COMPATIBILITY:

Integration and compatibility are key and If a solution doesn’t play nice with your current setup, it’s probably not worth your time.

FUNCTIONALITY:

The sole purpose of software and automation is to free up manpower to focus on things that require more creativity and involve less drudgery. If the solution needs a lot of engineering time to setup and build features, a closer look needs to be taken on whether the invested extra time is going to be worth the benefits.

Suppliers that offer pilot programs should be a first choice here as pilot programs are a great way to check just how feasible a certain solution is. A pilot program is like a test-run and involves running the program on a small scale to better understand the large scale implications.

PRICE:

: The most important factor is the price. How much a solution costs is just part of the picture, how much it costs in comparison to your current solution proves whether it is viable. A lot of people on a strict budget might want to look at price before anything else since if something’s outside the budget, there’s no point wasting time with it. In contrast, people with a more flexible budget feel price isn’t the most important factor.

CREDIBILITY:

If well established companies are already using the solutions you’re about to buy, that’s a major positive sign for you. It’s also good to look at companies within your sector that has worked with for them in the past, this could be a good indicator of whether the solution will be good for you or not.
